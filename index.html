The City of Joy
Historical Significance

Kolkata has a rich history, dating back to the arrival of the English East India Company in 1690. It played a crucial role during British colonial rule and was the capital of India until 1911. The city's history is marked by various cultural, social, and political movements.

Cultural Diversity

Kolkata is known for its vibrant cultural scene, including literature, art, music, and cinema. It has produced many renowned artists, writers, and intellectuals. The city celebrates various festivals with enthusiasm, and its cuisine is famous for its unique flavors.

Economic and Commercial Hub

Kolkata is an important economic and commercial center in India. It houses significant industries, including manufacturing, finance, and IT. The port of Kolkata is one of the oldest and busiest in the country, facilitating trade and commerce.

Most Visited Place

Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata, West Bengal, is a remarkable white marbled structure built to commemorate Queen Victoria's 25-year rule over India. It resembles the Victoria Memorial in London and is surrounded by a lush green garden with statues and sculptures. The 64-acre complex includes a 16-foot tall bronze statue of victory, enhancing its grandeur. Illuminated at night, the memorial's Sound and Light shows add to its charm. It's a must-visit place to experience the essence of the Victorian era in the modern world.

Fort William, Kolkata

Fort William, a grand structure in Kolkata, India, built in 1696, stands on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River. Named after King William III, it served as the British's first stronghold. The fort features meticulous stonework, arched windows, and lush green gardens. Over time, it had various functions, including being infamous as the 'black hole of Calcutta.' Today, it belongs to the Indian Army, housing up to 10,000 personnel and serving as the Eastern Command headquarters, limiting access to its interiors to army personnel and relatives. However, visitors can admire its stunning architecture.

Howrah Bridge, Kolkata

The iconic Howrah Bridge in Kolkata, also known as Rabindra Setu, is a massive steel cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River, connecting Howrah and Kolkata. It boasts a historic grandeur and is beautifully lit up at night. The bridge offers a spectacular view of the city, especially during a ferry ride between Kolkata and Howrah. Renamed in honor of Rabindranath Tagore, it spans about 1500 ft without using nuts or bolts, relying solely on rivets for its construction.

Belur Math, Kolkata

Belur Math in Kolkata, situated on the west bank of the Hooghly River, spans 40 acres and serves as the headquarters of Ramakrishna Math and Mission. It attracts people from various religions who seek peace and tranquility. The temple's remarkable architecture blends Hindu, Christian, and Islamic motifs, symbolizing unity among religions. Founded by Swami Vivekananda, the temple complex includes a museum and affiliated educational institutions. Tourists spend 3-4 hours here, but the profound experiences and sentiments linger for a lifetime.

How to Reach

Cultured, musical, food paradise, and futuristic are a few adjectives that are apt for Kolkata and its people. It is also regarded as India’s intellect and culture capital. This capital city of West Bengal is located by the Hoogly River and is one of the finest cultural and educational hubs. It is also known as the “City Of Joy” and is one of the marvelloustravel destinations.

The mouth-watering cuisines of Kolkata are a must try if you happen to be in the city. Its sweet delicacies rasgullas and sandesh are popular everywhere. These sweets are awesome, and you can carry them back home. The colonial era architecture, clubs, monuments, music, and poetry will truly impress you. Its sprawling gardens, historical monuments, Victorian buildings, and temples are a must visit. In short, you can say that Kolkata is a must visit place for those who love to explore new places.

1.By Air:- Kolkata Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport consists of a modern, six-level L-shaped terminal divided into domestic and international wings.

2.By Train:- Kolkata has an efficient network of railways and is well linked to cities across the23 country. Superfast trainssuch as Rajdhani Express and Shatabdi Express connect Kolkata from Delhi and nearby places. There are two major railway stations in the city – one is located in Howrah and the other at Sealdah.

3.By Road/Self Drive:- Kolkata is connected with most of the Indian cities by road. The National Highway number 2 and 6 connect the city with other cities and states in India. Kolkata has a broad network of government and private buses. The Calcutta State Transport Corporation (CSTC), Calcutta Tramways Company (CTC) and West Bengal Surface Transport Corporation (WBSTC) run regulars bus service in the city. The Esplanade Terminus in the heart of the city is the main bus terminus.
